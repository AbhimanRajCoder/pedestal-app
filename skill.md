# Market Case Simulator Skill

## Overview

This skill creates an interactive **Market Case Simulator** section for the Pedestal mobile learning app. Users experience real historical financial events and manage virtual investment portfolios during crisis timelines to learn financial decision-making through gamified simulations.

## Design Philosophy

**Pedestal App Theme:**
- Modern fintech UI with dark mode first
- Smooth gradients and rounded cards
- Subtle glow accents (blue, green, gold)
- Clean typography and minimal interface
- Gamified learning (Duolingo-style but for finance)
- Glass-morphism effects
- Subtle financial grid backgrounds

## Screen Structure

### Screen 1: Case Selection
- Scrollable grid of simulation case cards
- Each card displays: title, description, duration, difficulty, start button
- Premium card design with financial visuals (charts, candles, icons)

### Screen 2: Simulation Start
- Historical context introduction panel
- Starting capital display (₹10,00,000)
- Available assets list
- "Begin Simulation" CTA

### Screen 3: Simulation Interface
Three main panels:
1. **Portfolio Panel** - value, gain/loss, allocation chart, risk score
2. **Market News Timeline** - chronological event feed
3. **Decision Cards** - interactive investment choices

### Screen 4: Crisis Event
- Breaking news announcement
- Visual market crash animation
- Real-time portfolio impact display

### Screen 5: Final Results
- Performance dashboard
- Detailed scoring breakdown
- Educational insights
- Replay option

## Gamification Elements

- Leaderboard rankings
- Achievement badges (Crash Survivor, Risk Manager, Master Diversifier, Market Strategist)
- Replay functionality
- Progress tracking

## Historical Case Data

### Case 1: Harshad Mehta Scam (1992)

**Title:** "The Big Bull Scam"

**Description:** "India's largest stock market manipulation. Can you avoid the crash?"

**Duration:** 4 weeks simulation

**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐ Medium

**Historical Context:**
Harshad Mehta, known as the "Big Bull," manipulated the Indian stock market through bank receipt (BR) fraud. He artificially inflated stock prices, particularly in banking and ACC (Associated Cement Company), before the scam was exposed in April 1992.

**Starting Capital:** ₹10,00,000

**Available Assets:**
- Banking stocks (SBI, Bank of India, Canara Bank)
- ACC (Associated Cement Company)
- Other blue-chip stocks
- Government bonds
- Gold
- Cash/FD

**Simulation Timeline:**

**Week 1: Market Boom**
- Event 1: "Banking stocks rally 8% on strong investor sentiment"
- Event 2: "ACC stock price doubles in just 3 weeks"
- Event 3: "Harshad Mehta becomes a household name as the 'Big Bull'"
- Decision Point: Invest heavily in banking/ACC OR Stay cautious

**Week 2: Peak Euphoria**
- Event 1: "Sensex hits all-time high, investors pour money into market"
- Event 2: "Banking stocks continue upward momentum"
- Event 3: "Some analysts question the sustainability of the rally"
- Decision Point: Take profits and diversify OR Ride the momentum

**Week 3: Warning Signs**
- Event 1: "Unusual trading patterns noticed in banking stocks"
- Event 2: "Journalist Sucheta Dalal begins investigating irregularities"
- Event 3: "Market volatility increases, rumors circulate"
- Decision Point: Exit risky positions OR Hold and wait

**Week 4: The Crash**
- Event 1: "BREAKING: Harshad Mehta scam exposed! ₹5,000 crore fraud discovered"
- Event 2: "Banking stocks crash 30% in a single day"
- Event 3: "ACC plummets 50%, investors panic"
- Event 4: "Sensex falls 570 points, wiping out months of gains"
- Final Outcome: Market stabilizes at much lower levels

**Asset Performance:**
- Banking stocks: +40% peak → -35% final
- ACC: +120% peak → -60% final
- Bonds: +2% steady
- Gold: +5% (safe haven)
- Sensex: -12% overall

**Learning Objectives:**
- Recognize market manipulation signs
- Understand concentration risk
- Value of diversification during euphoria
- Safe haven assets during crashes

## Scoring System

### Portfolio Value Score (40%)
- Final portfolio value vs. starting capital
- S-curve scoring: -50% = 0 points, 0% = 50 points, +20% = 100 points

### Risk Management Score (25%)
- Diversification maintained throughout
- Maximum drawdown limited
- Appropriate use of safe havens

### Decision Quality Score (20%)
- Timing of major decisions
- Risk-appropriate choices
- Learning from warning signals

### Diversification Score (15%)
- Asset allocation balance
- Sector exposure limits
- Geographic diversification (if applicable)
